Connector Types

Select a cable for more information

USB Type-A

Standard rectangular USB connector found on most computers and devices.

USB Type-B

Square USB connector commonly used for printers and scanners.

USB Type-C

Reversible USB connector supporting high-speed data and power delivery.

Micro USB

Small USB connector used in many older smartphones and accessories.

Mini USB

Older, slightly larger USB connector used for cameras and peripherals.

HDMI

High-Definition Multimedia Interface for transmitting video and audio.

DisplayPort

Digital display interface for monitors and high-resolution video output.

VGA

Analog video connector used for older monitors and projectors.

DVI

Digital Visual Interface for video display connections.

RJ-45

Ethernet network cable connector for wired internet connections.

RJ-11

Phone line connector used for DSL and landline telephones.

3.5mm Audio

Standard headphone and microphone audio jack.

TOSLINK Optical

Fiber optic audio connector for high-quality sound systems.

eSATA

External Serial ATA connector for high-speed external storage devices.

Thunderbolt

High-speed data and display connector developed by Intel and Apple.

FireWire

IEEE 1394 high-speed interface used for video cameras and drives.

SD Card

Secure Digital memory card for cameras and portable devices.

microSD Card

Smaller version of SD card used in smartphones and compact devices.

Molex

4-pin power connector used in older PCs for drives and peripherals.

SATA Power

Power connector for modern internal hard drives and SSDs.

SATA Data

Data connector for internal storage devices.

PS/2

Legacy connector for keyboards and mice.

DB9 Serial

9-pin connector for serial communication devices.

Parallel Port

Legacy printer connector with 25 pins.
